Research on Mobile App Analysis and Testing

Mobile apps are typical GUI-centered and event-driven software. They are now ubiquitous and serving the needs of our daily life in many different aspects. However, due to the complex end-user environments (e.g., different OSes, vendor devices and third-party libraries), ensuring app reliability and correctness has thus become a longstanding challenge in industry. Our research aims to tackle this challenge by developing novel, effective and practical approaches and techniques to improve app quality, reliability and usability.

To this end, we have devoted much research effort over the recent years and developed several effective app analysis and testing techniques, including:
  • Stoat, a fully automated GUI fuzzing technique for finding crashing bugs;
  • Genie and SetDroid, fully automated GUI fuzzing techniques for finding non-crashing, logic bugs;
  • Themis, a ground-truth benchmark for evaluating automated GUI fuzzing techniques.

    • In addition to successfully finding many bugs in open-source apps, our techniques has found and reported 100+ bugs in several highly-popular industrial apps with billions of monthly-active users, many of which have been already fixed by the app vendors.
