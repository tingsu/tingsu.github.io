Ting Su is a Professor of Software Engineering Institute, East China Normal University, China (2020-). Previously, he was a postdoctoral scholar at ETH Zurich, Switzerland (2019-2020), in the group of Prof. Zhendong Su. He worked as a postdoctoral research fellow with Prof. Yang Liu in Cyber Security Lab (CSL), NTU, Singapore (2016-2019). He got his Ph.D in computer science from East China Normal University (ECNU), Shanghai, China (2011-2016), and was surpervised by Prof. Jifeng He and Prof. Geguang Pu. He also had one-year visit in University of California, Davis (UCD), USA (2014-2015), and was advised by Prof. Zhendong Su. He also worked as a software engineer intern in Synopsys, and a research scientist in Rolls-Royce@NTU Research Lab (2016-2017). He is a recipient of 国家海外青年人才计划.

His research focuses on developing effective methodologies, techniques and tools to improve software quality, reliability and security.

  • Stateful Systems/Software (mobile applications, network protocols, OS kernels, robotic systems)
  • Compilers, Program Analyzers and Deep Learning Systems
  • Symbolic Execution and Constraint Solving
  • Formal Verification

His research work received generous support from Chinese/Swiss NSF, Huawei, ByteDance, Ant Financial, Google, and won several ACM SIGSOFT Distinguished Paper Awards in top-tier venues (ICSE × 1, ASE × 4, ISSTA × 1), Google Faculty Research Award, ACM Student Research Competition Awards (First Place in ICSE'16; Second Place in ICSE'21), and Research Prototype Tool Competition Awards (First Places×2: ChinaSoft'23, NASAC'17). His research on symbolic execution has led to SmartRocket Unit, a commercial automated unit testing tool, which is serving many companies in China for testing industrial embedded software; his research on mobile app analysis and testing has led to several tools and dataset, e.g., Stoat (cited 460+), Themis, Kea/Kea2 and FastBot (starred 1100+). Specifically, Kea2 (and its idea) has been accepted and adopted by industry (e.g., WeChat's iExplorer, WeChat Payment(微信支付)'s UAT, and Huawei's DevEco Studio/Testing for HarmonyOS(鸿蒙系统)).

He is also actively developing a course (ecnu-sa-lab) on software testing, analysis and verification for undergraduate/graduate students.
